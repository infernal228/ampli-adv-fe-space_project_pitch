Black Hole

Scientists have hypothesized that our universe was a three-dimensional "packing" around the event horizon of a four-dimensional black hole. And if so, then our world appeared at the moment when the star of the four-dimensional universe fell into a black hole. In our three-dimensional universe, black holes have two-dimensional event horizons, that is, they are surrounded by a two-dimensional boundary that marks the "point of no return." In the case of the four-dimensional Universe, the black hole will have a three-dimensional event horizon. The researchers proposed a hypothesis according to which our Universe was never inside a singularity, but rather, was born beyond the bounds of the event horizon, protected from a singularity. It emerged from the remains of an exploding four-dimensional star.
